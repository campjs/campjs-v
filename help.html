Getting to CampJS IV

Free busses will get you to and from the CampJS venue. Busses will leave the airport around 2pm, then swing by Varsity Lakes Railway Station shortly thereafter. Varsity Lakes Station is on the Gold Coast line. If you want to be picked up from Varsity Lakes, be there by 2pm.

The pickup point at Gold Coast Airport will be outside the International Arrivals side of the airport, as marked on the map below. Note that Gold Coast Airport is tiny, you should not have trouble finding us. For early arrivals – people will be waiting at the pub.

Transport from CampJS IV

A single large bus will transport people to arrive at the airport to meet midday flights. Many people are driving down earlier and you may be able to catch a lift with them if you wish to depart more hastily, though we would appreciate any help straightening things out after the event.

You may find someone willing to carpool with you. Find people with available seats or advertise them on the flights/carpooling spreadsheet.

CampJS Start and End Times

The camp officially starts when the bus arrives on Friday, around 4:30pm, and ends Monday Morning around 9am. We are looking for volunteers to help set up on Friday. Volunteers may arrive any time after midday!

Accommodation

The venue has dormitory bunk rooms. Dorms have access to toilet and shower facilities via a covered walkway. Mattresses are provided, but you will need to provide your own linen, blanket and pillow. You are welcome to bring tents if you wish to camp!

Diets & Menu

Code of Conduct

CampJS attendees must read our code of conduct and know we are very serious about making sure CampJS is a safe and welcoming environment for everyone. We will not tolerate any sort of harassment or exclusionary activities. All attendees are expected to use their most mature, inclusive and least-prejudiced behaviour for the duration of the event.

If you'd like to report any issues, do not hesitate to approach Wei Lu or Tim Oxley. Alternatively, email us both at codeofconduct@campjs.com.

Things To Bring

Earplugs

I expect there to be noise late into night, and I can't guarantee people won't snore. If you want to sleep well, bring earplugs. I cannot stress this enough.

I personally sleep with earplugs every night and it is bliss for me and convenient for others who can get on with their lives without worrying about disturbing me. If you don't bring earplugs, I don't want to hear you complaining about noise.

Tools for coping with no Internet

Plan to have zero internet. Don't plan to have even a little. Zero.

Here are some essentials you may want to install/familiarise yourself with prior to CampJS IV:

  • node/npm (Installation guide by Benjamin Lupton)
  • npmd(gives you a local npm repo)
  • Google Chrome
  • git
  • Homebrew, if you're on OSX
  • Dash for OSX, great offline documentation viewer
  • A decent text editor. If you're not already using vim, then perhaps try Atom, SublimeText or something like WebStorm/WebMatrix
  • Windows users probably want to dual-boot Linux
